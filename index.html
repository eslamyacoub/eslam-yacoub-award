The Nominee

Dr. Eslam
Yacoub

Dr. Eslam Yacoub holds a Master's and PhD in Teaching English for Specific Purposes. Since joining Alamein International University (AIU) in 2024, he has designed and implemented the university's entire English language support infrastructure from the ground up.

When he joined AIU, he stepped into a bold experiment in Egyptian higher education. Founded in 2020 as a pioneering English-Medium Instruction institution, AIU faced a daunting linguistic reality: 85% of the 2025 incoming cohort had completed their secondary education entirely in Arabic. These students were enrolled in nine rigorous degree programs — Dentistry, Engineering, Computer Science and more — all taught in English.

He recognized that without robust support infrastructure, language would become a barrier to equity and success. He committed himself to building that bridge.

His nomination is fully supported by Alamein International University. The cumulative impact of his work led directly to the founding of the AIU English Learning Center in December 2025, which he now directs.

The Innovation Story

Seven Steps to a
Language Ecosystem

01

Diagnosing the Problem — AIU-STEP

Evidence that the gap was real and precisely measured

Dr. Yacoub designed the AIU Standardized Test of English Proficiency (AIU-STEP) from scratch — a comprehensive, CEFR-aligned instrument spanning four skills: Reading Comprehension (40%), Listening (20%), Grammar (30%), and Writing Analysis (10%), covering 120 items.

Key Finding: Of 2,727 incoming students, 1,218 (45%) placed at B1 or below — lacking proficiency for independent EMI engagement. Of these, 398 (15%) placed at A1–A2 (near-beginner). Without the AIU-STEP, the scale and urgency of the gap would have remained invisible.
02

Building the Foundation — English 1 & English 2

Purpose-built courses for AIU's unique student population

Based on AIU-STEP results, Dr. Yacoub designed and delivered two foundational pillars: LAN022 (English 1) for the 45% of students below B2, and LAN111 (English 2) for the 55% at or above B2. All 2,727 incoming students received targeted language support from day one.

03

Understanding Real Needs — The Needs Analysis

952 participants across students, faculty, and industry

To ensure the ESP curriculum was grounded in real student experience, Dr. Yacoub conducted a comprehensive needs analysis reaching 865 students across all nine programs, plus 64 faculty focus groups and 23 industry representatives — a total of 952 participants.

This layered approach captured both cross-disciplinary needs and the unique vocabulary and communication demands of each field, directly shaping every course book, glossary, and assessment task.

He also authored six bilingual English–Arabic disciplinary glossaries — covering Engineering, Mathematics, Basic Sciences, Business, Medical Purposes, and Arts & Design — distributed to all 2,727 first-year students as an immediate bridge while ESP curricula were being developed.
04

Discipline-Specific Support — The ESP Suite

Nine courses for nine programs, grounded in professional reality

Dr. Yacoub designed course books for each program. The Engineering course book spans eight thematic units — Architecture, Project Management, Electronics, Mechatronics, Oil & Gas, Biomedical Engineering, Materials Science, and Emerging Trends — concluding with a comprehensive A-to-Z glossary.

The Computer Science course book mirrors a modern CS education, covering Java, Python, cloud computing, machine learning, cybersecurity, and even the ethical dimensions of technology.

05

Practice Validated by Science — Five Publications

Five publications that carry AIU's story to the global ESP community

Every intervention — the AIU-STEP, the needs analysis, the ESP curricula, the Academic Writing course — has generated original scholarly evidence published in peer-reviewed international journals. AIU's institutional innovations become replicable, globally accessible models.

Publications span Q1 journals including the International Arab Journal of English for Specific Purposes, the American Journal of Medicine and Health Studies, and the International Journal of English for Specific Purposes (JOINESP), across 2025–2026.
06

Beyond the Classroom — Mentorship, Writing & Community

Engagement that extends far beyond formal teaching hours

Dr. Yacoub established a weekly Conversation Club and appointed 24 high-proficiency peer mentors across all programs. He dedicated over 320 office hours in 2025 to personalized writing and language consultations.

He also proposed and submitted the Alamein International Journal of Research (AIJR) — a quarterly, peer-reviewed journal dedicated to outstanding undergraduate research. Students who arrived unable to write a paragraph in English are now preparing manuscripts for its inaugural issue.

07

Measurable Impact — The English Learning Center

The culmination: an institution transformed

In December 2025, Dr. Yacoub's work culminated in the founding of the AIU English Learning Center, with himself as Founding Director. In its inaugural semester alone, the Center reached 310 students through two flagship services.

Conversation Club: 14 weekly sessions, 242 cumulative attendances, 19 permanent peer mentors.
Academic Writing Consultation Service: 68 individual one-to-one sessions supporting graduation projects and peer-review-ready research papers.
